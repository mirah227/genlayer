**Title: The Weight of Quiet Things**

In the hush between two heartbeats,
where even time forgets to breathe,
there lives a truth too soft for thunder—
a quiet thing we hardly see.

It lingers in unfinished sentences,
in eyes that turn but almost stay,
in hands that hover just an inch
from giving everything away.

The world is loud with breaking stories,
with blazing skies and falling kings,
but deeper still, beneath the noise,
we're shaped by small, unspoken things.

A look that lasts a second longer,
a name you almost dared to call,
the courage buried in your chest
that trembled—but stood tall.

Not every war is fought with fire,
not every loss is meant to show,
some battles bloom in silent rooms
where only you will ever know.

So do not curse the quiet moments,
or wish for louder, brighter signs—
for in the stillness, you are forged
by fragile, unseen, sacred lines.

And when the world demands your thunder,
and asks you what your strength may be,
remember all the quiet things
that taught your soul to breathe.
